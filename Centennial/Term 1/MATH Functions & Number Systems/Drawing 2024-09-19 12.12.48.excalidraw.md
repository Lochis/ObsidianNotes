## Text Elements
To write a repeating decimal as a fraction first move the decimal point to the right by multiplying by a power of 10 so that the repeating terms are directly to the right of the decimal. Have this new decimal equal n multiplied by the same power of 10. This will be equation 1. Then multiply both sides of equation 1 by a power of 10 depending on how many repeating decimal places there are. If there is 1 repeating digit multiply each side by​ 10, by 100 if 2 digits repeat and so on. This will be equation 2. Now subtract equation 1 from equation 2 and solve for n. ^zd1OSNxM
