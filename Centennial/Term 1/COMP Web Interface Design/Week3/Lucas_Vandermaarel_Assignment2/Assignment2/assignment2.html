Our world is rapidly changing. We need to do something about it.

We, people, are the threat to the biodiversity of our forests and the storing of our overbearing carbon emmissions. The International Union for Conservation of Nature and Natural Resources (Â© IUCN) is creating solutions to help with these issues.

The IUCN encourages the sustainance of natural resources and soil biodiversity by rewarding farmers for sustainable farming, create targets on national and global levels for us to rebalance our current ecological standing, and rebuild forests along with policies to on the destruction of certain forest areas. They have many more functions! These include the governing the livelihoods and sustainable use of wildlife to increase the chance of survival of endangered species, the managing of 8.16% of the global ocean, and the advising on nature as one of the named advisors to the World Heritage Committee.
