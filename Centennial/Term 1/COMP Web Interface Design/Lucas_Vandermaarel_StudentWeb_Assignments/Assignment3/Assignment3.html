Nissan GT-R

The Nissan GT-R is a car everyone should own. We built the R34 back in 1999 to 2002, encorporating advanced-at-the-time features like all-wheel-drive and a disaply screen that showed real-time driving information. Featuring in movies such as The Fast and Furious and video games such as Need for Speed, the R34 became a legend.

In 2007, Nissan launched a new GT-R. The R35. While the R34 was part of the Skyline family, the R35 was not. The R35 came with a 3.8-liter twin-turbo V6 engine which brought a great improvement in horsepower over the R34. During this time, it was known for it's speed. It had a 0 to 60mph time of just under 3 seconds.

By 2024, the GT-R had received even more upgrades, like better aerodynamics and new tech features. Special versions like the NISMO edition focus on extreme performance, keeping the GT-R's "Godzilla" nickname alive. It's still a car that offers incredible speed and handling without the super-high price of other sports cars.

Some classic features of GT-Rs:

  • All wheel drive

  • Niche driving information on the dash

  • High horsepower
