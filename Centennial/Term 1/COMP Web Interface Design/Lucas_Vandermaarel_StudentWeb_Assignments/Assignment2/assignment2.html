Our world is rapidly changing. We need to do something about it.

We, people, are the threat to the biodiversity of our forests and the storing of our overbearing carbon emmissions. The International Union for Conservation of Nature and Natural Resources (Â© IUCN) is creating solutions to help with these issues.

The IUCN encourages the sustainance of natural resources and soil biodiversity by rewarding farmers for sustainable farming, create targets on national and global levels for us to rebalance our current ecological standing, and rebuild forests along with policies to on the destruction of certain forest areas. They have many more functions! These include the governing the livelihoods and sustainable use of wildlife to increase the chance of survival of endangered species, the managing of 8.16% of the global ocean, and the advising on nature as one of the named advisors to the World Heritage Committee.

More information about the topic of Protect Areas and Land Use:

  • Land Use Planning Archives
      • The Land Use Planning Archives holds information about laws, governance planning, plans around emergencies, courses and other pieces of information regarding land use.

  • Canadian Agri-Food Policy Institute - Land Use Hub
      • The Canadian Argi-Food Policy Institute as it pertains to land, offers a multitude of resources about sustainable argiculture practices as well as an argiculture mapping tool.

  • Protected Planet
      • Protected Planet catalogues all of the protected areas in the world for you to see! You can search for an area based on their individual name, region, country, or OECM.

  • Canada's Parks & Protected Areas
      • Canada's Parks & Protected Areas will provide information about why protecting these places is important and provides a map of these protected areas.

  • Convention of Biological Diversity
      • The Convention of Biological Diversity usually has another map that they provide. However, they speak about the importance of keeping biodiversity flourishing and their work on preventing biodiversity disasters from happening.

  • Ontario Land Use Planning
      • The provincial government of Ontario provides information regarding land use. Important policies, laws, processes, and the overall vision that the Ontario government has can be found here.
